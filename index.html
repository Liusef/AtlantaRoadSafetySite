The Data

The data for this project comes from the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT)'s Georgia Crash Data Dashboard, which contains information about all reported accidents in Georgia from 2019 to 2023. To make the size of the dataset more manageable, we only looked at accidents that occurred in Atlanta in 2023, which still left us with almost 35,000 data points.

Environmental Factors

Environmental factors like road conditions and visibility are common concerns for drivers, and can vary seasonally due to weather changes and reduced daylight hours in winter. Plotting the amount of accidents reported under different conditions shows that the majority of accidents occur under "safe" driving conditions (dry roads and in daylight) all year round. While there is an increase in the number of accidents occuring on wet roads or in the dark in the winter / early spring months, the total number of accidents in these months is not significantly different from the number of accidents in other parts of the year. This suggests that more accidents occur under these conditions in the winter and spring simply because these conditions are more common in the winter and spring. While drivers may percieve driving in the winter as more dangerous, the number of accidents that occur in the winter / spring months seems to be about the same, so the time of year doesn't seem to have an effect on the total number of accidents.
